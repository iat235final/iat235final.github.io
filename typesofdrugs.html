Common Drug Classifications

CANNABIS

Common types: Hashish, Edibles, Oil, Marijuana
Effect: Depressant, Relaxing, Sedative
Cannabis, also known as marijuana among other names, is a psychoactive drug from the Cannabis plant used for medical or recreational purposes. The main psychoactive part of cannabis is tetrahydrocannabinol.

HEROIN

Common types: Black tar Heroin, Fentanyl, Oil, Carfentanyl
Effect: Euphoric, Relaxing, Numbing
Heroin is an illegal, highly addictive drug processed from morphine, a naturally occurring substance extracted from the seed pod of certain varieties of poppy plants. It is typically sold as a white or brownish powder that is "cut" with sugars, starch, powdered milk, or quinine.

COCAINE

Common types: Base form (crack), cocaine hydrochloride
Effect: Stimulant, Euphoric, Reduced Appetite
Cocaine, also known as coke, is a strong stimulant most frequently used as a recreational drug. It is commonly snorted, inhaled as smoke, or dissolved and injected into a vein. Mental effects may include loss of contact with reality, an intense feeling of happiness, or agitation.

AMPHETAMINES

Common types: Methamphetamine, Adderall, Ritalin
Effect: Stimulant, Focus, Paranoia
Amphetamine is a central nervous system stimulant that is used in the treatment of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, narcolepsy, and obesity. Methamphetamine is an addictive, potent central nervous system stimulant that is mainly used as a recreational drug.

PSYCHEDLICS

Common types: MDMA, LSD, Psilocybin
Effect: Euphoric, Hallucinogenic, Stimulant
Psychedelic drugs, also known as hallucinogenic drugs, or simply hallucinogens, are a group of substances, including chemicals, such as LSD, and plants, such as peyote, that are usually used recreationally, to change and enhance sensory perceptions, thought processes, energy levels, and to facilitate spiritual experiences.
